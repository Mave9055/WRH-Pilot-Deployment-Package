# Safety & Participation Rules

## Participant Version (Read in Session 00)

> **1. No Introductions. No Forced Sharing.**  
> In this program, we do **not** “go around the room.” You will not be asked to stand up, give your name, or explain why you are here.
> - You will never be required to talk about your trauma, charges, addiction, or family.  
> - You can pass on any question at any time.  
> - You can stay completely silent for all 30 sessions and still be doing this right.

> **2. This is Psychoeducation, Not Group Therapy.**  
> We study how the nervous system and trauma patterns work, using one documented case study (Daniel) as the map. You are not here to perform your pain; you are here to **understand your system**.

> **3. Your Nervous System Leads.**  
> - If you feel overwhelmed or flooded, you may step out without asking permission.  
> - Yellow/Red Light safety protocols are in place to keep the room regulated and to route true crisis to the right support.
> - You are not “in trouble” for needing space. Your body is doing its job.

> **4. The “Plan B” for Terror and Focus Problems.**  
> If being in this room feels like terror, or you can’t focus:  
> - Ground once: feet on the floor, one slow exhale.  
> - Drop performance: you are not here to impress anyone.  
> - Catch one sentence: just hold on to one idea that lands today.  
> - Write one word about it later. That is enough for this session.

> **5. The Only Rule About Other People’s Stories.**  
> - No crosstalk, no advice-giving, no “fixing” anyone.  
> - If someone else’s story hits you, that is information about your system. We’ll teach you what to do with that.

---
*“If you never say a word, but you keep showing up and quietly stealing one sentence per session, this program is working for you.”*

## Federal/Clinical Summary Paragraph

**Safety & Participation Model:** WRH is a non-exposure, non-processing psychoeducation curriculum; participants are never required to introduce themselves to the group or disclose personal histories. The program explicitly eliminates “go-around-the-room” introductions and forced sharing, and instead uses standardized safety protocols (Yellow/Red Light), micro-regulation scripts, and an opt-in participation model to accommodate veterans, justice-involved adults, and individuals in addiction treatment who often experience severe hyperarousal and concentration problems in group settings. Participants may remain silent for the entire course while still engaging via structured noticing tasks and written reflections, which supports both psychological safety and long-term retention of key concepts.
