# Pilot Addendum: Stepping Stones Recovery of Arkansas

## Header / Purpose
This addendum outlines the specific deployment plan for the **What Really Happened (WRH) Trauma Psychoeducation Program** at **Pilot Site A: Stepping Stones Recovery of Arkansas**. This pilot serves as a critical proof-of-concept for the WRH curriculum in a residential recovery environment, focusing on veteran suicide prevention and the restoration of executive function for individuals rebuilding their lives.

## Site-Specific Context
**Pilot Site A: Stepping Stones Recovery of Arkansas**  
*Location:* 1561 County Road 2305, Hartman, AR  
*Type:* 501(c)(3) non-profit transitional recovery community organization.  
*Certifications:* NARR certified and Arkansas Department of Community Correction licensed.  

Stepping Stones provides peer-supported services, recovery housing, and volunteerism for Johnson and Franklin counties. WRH fits this environment by providing non-therapeutic, analytical psychoeducation that complements existing peer support without requiring clinical disclosure.

## Pilot Objectives
- **Feasibility:** Successfully deliver the 30-session WRH curriculum in a residential recovery setting.
- **Retention:** Demonstrate that participants retain and apply key concepts (e.g., `The Hum`, `Pattern Veto`) beyond the 20% baseline.
- **Safety:** Maintain strict non-disclosure and non-processing standards to ensure psychological safety.
- **Data Collection:** Gather lightweight operational data to support future federal contracting and expansion.

## Proposed Schedule & Logistics
- **Format:** 30-session cohort (75 minutes each).
- **Frequency:** 2–3 sessions per week (approx. 10–15 weeks).
- **Cohort Size:** Recommended 8–15 participants.
- **Delivery:** Facilitator-led or hybrid digital (via the WRH Web Portal).
- **Timeline:** Proposed ramp-up for May–June 2026.

## Safety & Risk Management
The WRH pilot at Stepping Stones will prioritize voluntary participation and choice in engagement.
- **Grounding Checkpoints:** Every 15–20 minutes to maintain room regulation.
- **Clinical Coordination:** Facilitators will coordinate with on-site Stepping Stones staff for any emerging clinical needs.
- **Warm-Handoff Protocols:** Use of licensed providers, local VA resources, or the Veterans Crisis Line (988, press 1) as needed.
- **Non-Disciplinary:** Attendance is not tied to disciplinary consequences; the program remains strictly psychoeducational.

## Retention & Outcomes Tracking
To address the "learn 20, forget 80" problem, the pilot includes:
- **Spaced Boosters:** Regular review of core concepts.
- **Commitment Tools:** One-tool commitment cards for daily application.
- **30-Day Follow-up:** Simple metrics to track durable tool usage.
- **Metrics:** Pre/post confidence scales, tool usage rates, and qualitative feedback on "Mirror Moments."

## Roles & Responsibilities
- **Capitol Contracts LLC:** Provides trained facilitation, all program materials (manuals, Plan B cards), and data analysis.
- **Stepping Stones Recovery:** Provides consistent meeting space, participant recruitment, and a staff liaison for crisis coordination.

## Success Criteria & Next Steps
Success is defined by high cohort retention (80%+ completion) and self-reported application of at least three WRH tools in daily life. Data from this pilot will be used to refine the WRH Deployment Package for broader deployment in jail pods and VA-funded programs.
