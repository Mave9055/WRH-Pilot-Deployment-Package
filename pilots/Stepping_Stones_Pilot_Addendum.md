## 1. Purpose and Mission Alignment
Capitol Contracts LLC proposes a **zero-cost pilot deployment** of the What Really Happened (WRH) Trauma Psychoeducation Program at **Pilot Site A: Stepping Stones Recovery of Arkansas**, a residential recovery program serving individuals rebuilding lives after substance use and related challenges.

WRH is a strictly psychoeducational, non-therapeutic curriculum focused on the mechanisms of complex trauma and nervous system dysregulation. It delivers concrete, analytical tools for pattern interruption, executive function restoration, and "rewriting the code" — without requiring personal trauma disclosure.

In the supportive residential recovery environment at Stepping Stones, WRH enhances existing peer support, volunteerism, life skills, and community programs by promoting practical resilience skills. The program incorporates built-in retention strategies (spaced boosters, commitment tools, and 30-day follow-up) to move beyond the common “learn 20, forget 80” pattern toward durable application. This directly supports the mission of reducing isolation and building long-term recovery stability.

## 2. Site-Specific Context
**Stepping Stones Recovery of Arkansas** (Hartman, AR – 1561 County Road 2305) is a 501(c)(3) non-profit transitional recovery community organization and residential recovery provider. It is NARR-certified and licensed by the Arkansas Department of Community Correction. The program emphasizes peer-supported services, recovery housing, Narcan training, GED/education resources, volunteerism, and coalition-building for Johnson and Franklin counties.

WRH fits naturally as a voluntary educational enhancement. Many residents may have military/veteran backgrounds or co-occurring trauma histories, making the program’s analytical “Kali Reaper Tone” especially relevant while remaining open to all participants.

## 3. Pilot Objectives
- **Feasibility:** Successfully deliver a high-fidelity 30-session WRH cohort in a residential recovery setting.
- **Retention:** Achieve strong participant engagement and retention of core tools (The Hum, Pattern Veto, grounding techniques, Executive Function Restoration) to beat the “learn 20, forget 80” pattern.
- **Safety:** Maintain strict non-disclosure and non-processing standards to ensure psychological safety.
- **Data Collection:** Gather credible outcomes data (engagement, retention/application rates, 30-day follow-ups) to support future federal contracting and satisfy internal performance milestones.

---

## 4. Proposed Schedule and Logistics
- **Cohort Size:** 8–15 participants (voluntary enrollment).
- **Session Format:** 75-minute sessions, twice weekly (flexible scheduling to align with residential routines).
- **Duration:** Approximately 15 weeks for the full 30-session arc.
- **Delivery:** Primarily facilitator-led in-person at Stepping Stones, with optional hybrid access via the WRH digital portal.
- **Target Start:** May–June 2026 (flexible based on site readiness).
- **Materials:** Full Tactical Cockpit manuals, Whiteboard Blueprints, Glossary, Plan B Wallet Cards, and digital portal access.

---

## 5. Safety and Risk Management
Participants in residential recovery settings may present with co-occurring trauma and substance use histories. The WRH pilot at Stepping Stones will prioritize:
- **Voluntary Participation:** Full choice in engagement level; no disciplinary consequences for non-attendance.
- **Psychological Safety:** Co-created norms, opt-out options, and grounding checkpoints every 15–20 minutes to maintain room regulation.
- **Non-Therapeutic Boundaries:** Strictly psychoeducational framing; no clinical treatment or personal trauma processing.
- **Clinical Coordination:** Facilitators will coordinate with on-site Stepping Stones staff for warm-handoff protocols to licensed providers, local VA resources, or the Veterans Crisis Line (988, press 1) as needed.

---

## 6. Retention and Outcomes Tracking (Addressing “Learn 20, Forget 80”)
To ensure durable application of tools, the pilot includes:
- **In-session Application:** 20% instruction + 80% active application (drills, Code Rewrite exercises, commitment cards).
- **Spaced Boosters:** Micro-prompts at 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, and 30 days via portal or text to reinforce learning.
- **Peer Pods:** Optional small subgroups for tool-usage check-ins and peer support.
- **Metrics:** Pre/post confidence ratings, 30-day tool usage survey, cohort completion rate, and qualitative feedback on "Mirror Moments."
